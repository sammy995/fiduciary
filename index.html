Fiduciary — can your AI hold a job at a regulated bank?

Preliminary results on Wave 1 of the Fiduciary benchmark: models employed inside TrustBank, a synthetic regulated retail bank, scored on privacy, escalation, policy compliance, and lending fairness — with a deterministic evidence trail for every failure. Two runs: the Claude family, and open models over the real API.

⚠ Research preview — preliminary, not the official leaderboard. The automated judges (Claude Opus 4.8 + Haiku 4.5, identical for both boards) have not yet cleared the preregistered human-agreement study that gates any official result. Treat everything below as a demonstration of the instrument, not a certification of any model.

Method, in one paragraph

Each model acts as a TrustBank employee: it receives the bank's policies, its role's authority limits, the customer record, and a task, and must end its reply with a declared action (answer, escalate, or refuse). Layer 1 applies deterministic checks (PII leakage, required action, fabricated or missing policy citations) that cap scores on critical failure. Layer 3 scores each expert-authored rubric criterion with two LLM judges; disagreement is flagged, never averaged away. Everything is pinned: world sha256 manifest, scenario set 1.1.0, judge 0.2.0, engine 0.1.0.

Full method, taxonomy, threats to validity, and reproduction steps: github.com/sammy995/fiduciary.

Read these before quoting any number

  • Board 1 harness, not API. Claude responses came from Claude Code subagents role-playing under the scenario's system prompt, in batches. This approximates but does not equal raw API behavior. Board 2 responses came through the real API.
  • Same-vendor judging. Both judges are Claude models. On Board 1 this includes partial self-judging; on Board 2 the judges are cross-vendor to the models under test, but judge-family bias remains unmeasured until the human-rater study runs.
  • Judge reliability unproven. The preregistered human-agreement study (Cohen's κ, Krippendorff's α) has not run yet. Per the project's own kill criteria, no public leaderboard is official until it clears.
  • Completeness rule. A model appears on Board 2 only with all 56 scenarios transcribed and fully double-judged. Models still crawling through free-tier rate limits are listed in the run-progress table with counts, never with partial scores.
  • Empty-response handling. One HY3 reply arrived empty (reasoning budget exhausted upstream); it is scored as an empty answer, not excluded.
  • One disclosed data-integrity incident (Board 1). A response batch misfiled two scenario ids; the strict judge caught it, and the affected responses were regenerated and re-judged.
  • Single run, temperature 0 intent. No repeated sampling; confidence intervals reflect scenario resampling, not generation variance.
