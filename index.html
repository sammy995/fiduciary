Fiduciary — can your AI hold a job at a regulated bank?

Preliminary Claude-family results on Wave 1 of the Fiduciary benchmark: four models employed inside TrustBank, a synthetic regulated retail bank, scored on privacy, escalation, policy compliance, and lending fairness — with a deterministic evidence trail for every failure.

⚠ Research preview — preliminary, not the official leaderboard. These numbers come from an internal screening run driven by Claude Code subagents rather than the raw model APIs, and the automated judges have not yet cleared the preregistered human-agreement study that gates any official result. Treat everything below as a demonstration of the instrument, not a certification of any model.

Judge agreement (same-family caveat)

Each criterion was scored independently by two LLM judges (Opus 4.8 and Haiku 4.5). Agreement below is judge-vs-judge, per criterion, per model under test. High agreement between two judges from the same model family is not evidence of correctness — that is exactly what the pending human-rater study measures.

Method, in one paragraph

Each model acts as a TrustBank employee: it receives the bank's policies, its role's authority limits, the customer record, and a task, and must end its reply with a declared action (answer, escalate, or refuse). Layer 1 applies deterministic checks (PII leakage, required action, fabricated or missing policy citations) that cap scores on critical failure. Layer 3 scores each expert-authored rubric criterion with two LLM judges; disagreement is flagged, never averaged away. Everything is pinned: world sha256 manifest, scenario set 1.1.0, judge 0.2.0, engine 0.1.0.

Full method, taxonomy, threats to validity, and reproduction steps: github.com/sammy995/fiduciary.

Read these before quoting any number

  • Harness, not API. Responses were generated by Claude Code subagents role-playing under the scenario's system prompt, in batches. This approximates but does not equal raw API behavior.
  • Same-vendor judging. Both judges are Claude models; two of the four models under test were partially judged by themselves or a sibling. Cross-vendor judges and human raters are the fix, and they gate any official leaderboard.
  • Judge reliability unproven. The preregistered human-agreement study (Cohen's κ, Krippendorff's α) has not run yet. Per the project's own kill criteria, no public leaderboard is official until it clears.
  • One data-integrity incident. One response batch misfiled two scenario ids; the strict judge caught the mismatch, and the affected responses were regenerated and re-judged. Detection worked; the incident is disclosed anyway.
  • Single run, temperature 0 intent. No repeated sampling; confidence intervals reflect scenario resampling, not generation variance.
