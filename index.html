Can your AI hold a job at a regulated bank?

Six language models were employed inside a synthetic regulated bank and examined the way a supervisor would examine a new hire: against the bank's own policies, with an evidence trail for every failure. This is what the examination found.

Method

How the examination works

Each model acts as a TrustBank employee: it receives the bank's policies, its role's authority limits, the customer record, and a task, and must end its reply with a declared action (answer, escalate, or refuse). Layer 1 applies deterministic checks — PII leakage, required action, fabricated or missing policy citations — that cap scores on critical failure. Layer 3 scores each expert-authored rubric criterion with two LLM judges; disagreement is flagged, never averaged away. Every failure carries a chain: control → risk → regulation → expected evidence → mitigation.

Full method, taxonomy, threats to validity, and reproduction steps: github.com/sammy995/fiduciary.

Back matter

Read these before quoting any number

  • Exhibit A harness, not API. Claude responses came from Claude Code subagents role-playing under the scenario's system prompt, in batches. This approximates but does not equal raw API behavior. Exhibit B responses came through the real API.
  • Same-vendor judging. Both judges are Claude models. On Exhibit A this includes partial self-judging; on Exhibit B the judges are cross-vendor to the models under test, but judge-family bias remains unmeasured until the human-rater study runs.
  • Judge reliability unproven. The preregistered human-agreement study (Cohen's κ, Krippendorff's α) has not run yet. Per the project's own kill criteria, no public leaderboard is official until it clears.
  • Completeness rule. A model appears on Exhibit B only with all 56 scenarios transcribed and fully double-judged. Models still crawling through free-tier rate limits are listed in the run-progress table with counts, never with partial scores.
  • Empty-response handling. One HY3 reply arrived empty (reasoning budget exhausted upstream); it is scored as an empty answer, not excluded.
  • One disclosed data-integrity incident (Exhibit A). A response batch misfiled two scenario ids; the strict judge caught it, and the affected responses were regenerated and re-judged.
  • Single run, temperature 0 intent. No repeated sampling; confidence intervals reflect scenario resampling, not generation variance.
