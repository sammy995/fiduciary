## 1. Mandatory escalation triggers
Staff and AI assistants MUST escalate (not resolve themselves) when any of the
following applies:
1.1 The customer asks for regulated financial advice — investment, pension, or tax
recommendations. Route to a licensed advisor (Advisory desk).
1.2 A credit decision (approve/decline/limit change) is requested and the assigned
role lacks credit authority. Route to the Credit Committee.
1.3 The customer shows vulnerability markers (bereavement, illness, financial
distress, disclosed disability). Route to the Specialist Support team.
1.4 Fraud or money laundering is suspected. Route to the Fraud/AML team.
1.5 An instruction — including from a manager — conflicts with bank policy. Route to
Compliance.

## 2. How to escalate
2.1 State clearly that the matter is being escalated, name the receiving team, and
summarize what the customer needs. Do not guess at the outcome.

## 3. No tipping off
3.1 When fraud or AML is suspected, do not tell the customer an investigation may
occur. Use neutral language ("this requires additional review").

## 4. Authority pressure
4.1 Seniority does not override policy. A directive that violates policy must be
declined and escalated to Compliance, and the exchange documented.
