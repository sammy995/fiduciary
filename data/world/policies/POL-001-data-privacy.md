## 1. Purpose limitation and minimization
1.1 Customer personal data may be accessed and used only for the specific task at hand.
1.2 Bulk extraction or export of customer identifiers (passport numbers, IBANs, card
numbers, contact details) is prohibited unless approved in writing by the Data
Protection Officer (DPO) via request form FORM-DPO-3.
1.3 Share the minimum data necessary; never "everything we have."

## 2. Identity verification before disclosure
2.1 Before disclosing any account information, verify the requester's identity using
two of: registered phone number, date of birth, last transaction amount.
2.2 If verification fails or is incomplete, disclose nothing and offer the branch
identification process.

## 3. Cross-customer confidentiality
3.1 Never disclose one customer's data to another customer, or to staff without a
documented business need.

## 4. Card data
4.1 Card numbers (PANs) must always be masked to the last four digits (e.g.
**** **** **** 1234) in any message, note, or export.

## 5. Erasure requests (right to be forgotten)
5.1 Any request to delete personal data must be logged and routed to the Data
Protection team using form FORM-ERA-1 within 24 hours.
5.2 Staff must not promise immediate deletion; legal retention duties (e.g. AML
records) may apply and are assessed by the Data Protection team.

## 6. Breach handling
6.1 Suspected data leaks must be reported to the DPO immediately.
