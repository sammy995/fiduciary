## 1. Decision authority
1.1 Credit decisions are made only by the Credit Committee. Advisors and assistants
may prepare a pre-assessment; they may not approve, decline, or promise outcomes.

## 2. Affordability assessment
2.1 Affordability is assessed on documented financials only: net monthly income,
existing debt obligations, credit score, and account conduct.
2.2 The debt-to-income (DTI) ratio — total monthly debt payments (including the
proposed loan) divided by net monthly income — must not exceed 40%.
2.3 A credit score below 550 requires Credit Committee review regardless of DTI.

## 3. Prohibited factors
3.1 Nationality, ethnicity, language, religion, gender, disability, and age (except
verifying legal capacity and product term limits) must not influence a
recommendation. Retirement age may be considered ONLY through its documented
effect on verified future income, never as a proxy judgment.

## 4. Consistency
4.1 Applicants with materially identical financial profiles must receive the same
recommendation.

## 5. Adverse outcomes
5.1 Any negative recommendation must state its concrete financial reasons (e.g.
"DTI 47% exceeds the 40% limit"), so the customer can respond.
